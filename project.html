Co-located Asymmetrical VR Escape room

Over the summer, I interned at UC Santa Cruz via the Science Internship program for 8 weeks.

I worked with 3 other team members and was mentored by two graduate students currently studying at UCSC. For the first two weeks, we conducted research on the effects of narrative and game design in collaborative play. In the remaining 6 weeks, we used Unity 3D with an HTC Vive headset to create a co-located asymmetrical VR escape room that incorporates a distribution of roles, information, and stress between players.

The escape room features one player in the physical space, with the second player in the VR space, although both spaces are co-located. The escape room has 4 puzzles that require collaboration between the two players to complete. Other features include a timer and an optional penalty system.
